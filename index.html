What Is a CTF?

A Capture The Flag (CTF) is not merely a competition‚Äîit is a crucible where cyber warriors are forged. In this digital battlefield, teams face real-world security challenges, hunting for hidden flags through exploitation, cryptanalysis, forensic investigation, and tactical thinking.

Unlike theoretical exercises, CTFs demand hands-on combat with live systems, encrypted messages, vulnerable applications, and concealed secrets. Only those who combine technical prowess with strategic thinking shall prevail.

Guidelines & Rules

Eligibility

  • The competition is open to all UG and PG students currently enrolled in recognized Indian institutions. However, for students of NFSU Goa Campus, participation is limited to the following programs:
  • B.Sc.‚ÄìM.Sc. Forensic Science (Integrated) ‚Äì All semesters
  • M.Sc. Forensic Science ‚Äì All semesters
  • M.Sc. Cyber Security ‚Äì Semester 2
  • M.Sc. DFIS (Digital Forensic & Information Security) ‚Äì Semester 2
  • M.Tech AIDS ‚Äì Semester 2
  • Participants must register as a team of 1 to 3 members.
  • Each participant must be part of only one team; no substitutions are allowed once the registration has been done.
  • Valid College ID cards are mandatory for all participating teams.

Registration & Support

  • Registration will only be considered complete only after filling the Google Form (LINK).
  • Registration Fees Rs. 300/- + 18% GST must be paid via the link provided in Google Form. Failing which registration will not be recognised.
  • The official Discord server is the primary channel for all announcements and technical support (https://discord.gg/naxy8RZB).
  • Support requests must be raised via the designated ticket system; personal messages (DMs) to organizers are strictly prohibited.
  • By registering, you consent to the collection and use of personal data for verification and event communication.

Event Structure

  • The Online Qualifier will be conducted on 28 March, 2026, starting at 10:30 AM IST for a duration of 24 hours.
  • The format is Jeopardy-style CTF with categories including RevEng, Web, Crypto, OSINT, Forensics, AI/ML, Miscellaneous & more.
  • Based on the leaderboard at end of the event time, the Top 25 teams will be shortlisted for verification.
  • Verified teams will receive official invite letters for the Offline Finals by April 2, 2026.

Write-up Submission

  • Shortlisted teams must submit detailed solution write-ups within 8 hours of the round ending to prove legitimacy.
  • Write-ups must clearly explain the approach and methodology used; plagiarized or shared write-ups will lead to immediate disqualification.
  • Failure to submit write-ups within the deadline will result in disqualification for the final round invitation.

Fair Play & Technical Boundaries

  • Sharing flags or solutions between teams is a major violation and will result in disqualification for all parties involved.
  • Attacks against the scoreboard, registration servers, or event infrastructure (DDoS/DoS) are strictly prohibited.
  • Automated scanners (e.g., SQLMap, DirBuster) and login brute-forcing are not permitted unless explicitly required by a challenge.
  • The standard flag format is Kaal{s0m3_t3xt_h3r3} unless specified otherwise.

Accommodation & Travel

  • All expenses related to travel and stay for the offline finals must be handled by the participants.
  • The organizers will not provide accommodation facilities or travel reimbursements.

General Rules

  • The decision of the organizers regarding rule violations, scoring, and disputes is final and binding.
  • Any abusive, problematic, or toxic behaviour toward participants or staff will result in immediate disqualification.
  • Participation implies full acceptance of these rules and any official updates communicated during the event.
